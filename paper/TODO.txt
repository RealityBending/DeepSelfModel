1. Reframe the central claim
Current implicit claim

The paper often sounds like:

"Active inference implies these six Ultra-Priors."

A careful reviewer will immediately ask:

Why six?

and

Why these six rather than seven or ten?

This is currently the weakest point.

Replace with

State explicitly:

We propose six candidate latent dimensions that appear sufficient to explain a large fraction of personality variation under an active-inference framework.

Then later:

Future work may show that some dimensions should be merged, split, or replaced.

This sounds weaker rhetorically but stronger scientifically.

3. Add a "Why these six?" justification

This section is currently missing.

You need an explicit derivation argument.

We could mention in a few sentences tha a complete adaptive agent must answer six questions
World
Is the world predictable? (Volatility)
Is sensory information reliable? (Noise)
Body & Action
Do I have sufficient resources? (Vitality)
Can my actions change outcomes? (Tractability)
Value & Time
Are desirable states attainable? (Reward)
Is the future worth investing in? (Horizon)

Then argue that these dimensions correspond to orthogonal classes of uncertainty relevant to adaptive control.


4. Make Core Affective States mathematically necessary

Currently:

Ultra-Priors
→
Core Affective States
→
Precision Biases

looks elegant but somewhat arbitrary.

A reviewer will ask:

Why exactly these three affective states?

You need a stronger rationale.

For example:

Epistemic Arousal

Tracks uncertainty.

Agentic Engagement

Tracks controllability.

Existential Security

Tracks long-horizon viability.

Now show that each corresponds to one major functional domain:

Function	Affective State
Learning	Epistemic
Acting	        Agentic
Persisting	Existential

This would make the layer feel less invented.


6. Explicitly position tractability as "the expected controllability of state transitions." (language comes directly from computational psychiatry)

7. The most important conceptual revision

I would seriously consider changing the narrative from:

"Personality traits emerge from precision biases."

to

"Personality traits emerge from stable attractor geometries in the agent's generative model."

Why?

Because:

active inference is fundamentally about generative models
precision is only one component
reviewers often criticise precision-centric theories

The attractor framing is deeper and harder to attack.
